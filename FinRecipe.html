Black Pomfret Curry

Body firm, very deep, oval, and compressed. Operculum absent; gill opening reduced to a vertical slit on the side of the body; gill membrane broadly united to isthmus. Dorsal and anal fins preceded by a series of 5 to 10 blade-like spines with anterior and posterior points.

Dinner

Cooking time: 1 hr

Serves: 2

  •     Black Pomfret Medium – 4

  •     oil –2 tsp

  •     semolina – 1/2 cup

  •     Gralic cloves – 3

  •     Ginger – 1 inch

  •     Fennel seeds – 1 tsp

  •     Black peppercorns – 4

  •     Cloves – 3

  •     Turmeric powder – 1/2 tsp

  •     Dry red chillies – 4

  •     Small Tamarind– 1

  •     Fresh Corriander leaves – 1/2 cup

  •     Salt as per taste

1.Here’s how you can make one of the best Machi Recipes:

2. Place the fish steaks on a plate and sprinkle some salt. Mix and set aside for 5 minutes.

3.Add garlic cloves, ginger, fennel seeds, black peppercorns, cloves, turmeric powder, dry red chillies, tamarind, and coriander leaves in a mixer grinder. Grind them well.

4.Add about 3 tbsp of water to the masala mix to make a smooth batter.

5.Add salt into the spice mix at the end and blend well.

6 .Evenly coat the fish steaks with the spice batter and marinate for another 15 minutes.

7.Heat oil in a pan. Apply the marinated pomfret steaks with semolina nicely on both sides and place them carefully over the hot oil. Fry on medium heat on each side for about 3 minutes.

8.Place the fish pieces on a plate and garnish with more coriander leaves, lemon juice and a salad.
